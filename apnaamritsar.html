How To Reach Amritsar:

By air:

The Sri Guru Ram Das Jee International Airport in Amritsar, which is about 11 kilometres from the city centre, offers more than 150+ domestic and international flights each day. Daily direct flights are available from Delhi, Mumbai, Kuala Lumpur, Doha, London, etc.

View on Google Maps

By train:

The Amritsar Railway Station provides connections for the city to almost all of the important cities such as Delhi, Ahmedabad, Mumbai, Hyderabad, Agra & Kolkata. It takes around six hours to reach Amritsar from Delhi by rail.

View on Google Maps

By road:

Because it is situated on the National Highway 1, Amritsar has excellent road access. There are daily bus services to and from Ambala, Patiala, Delhi, Chandigarh, and Jammu. These services are conducted by both public and private companies. You can hire rickshaws or taxis to go about the city.

About our City

Welcome to Amritsar, a city steeped in history, culture, and spirituality. Located in the northwestern state of Punjab, India, Amritsar is renowned for its iconic landmarks, rich traditions, and warm hospitality.

History: Amritsar traces its roots back to the 16th century when Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru, founded the city around the sacred pool of the Golden Temple. Over the centuries, Amritsar has been witness to pivotal moments in Indian history, including the Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919 and the partition of India in 1947.

Top local Attractions Like:Hall Bazaar

Hall Bazaar in Amritsar is a bustling marketplace filled with colorful sights and vibrant energy. It's a lively hub where locals and tourists alike come to shop, eat, and soak in the lively atmosphere. The market is filled with a variety of stalls selling everything from traditional Indian clothing and accessories to spices, jewelry, and souvenirs. You can find delicious street food here too, from savory snacks to sweet treats. As you wander through the narrow lanes, you'll encounter the sounds of vendors calling out their wares and the aroma of freshly cooked food wafting through the air. Hall Bazaar offers a true taste of Amritsar's culture and is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the city.

Local Transports of Amritsar :

Auto Rickshaws (Tuk-Tuks):

Auto rickshaws are a popular mode of transportation in Amritsar. They are convenient for short distances and can usually accommodate up to three passengers. Make sure to negotiate the fare before starting your journey, as auto rickshaw drivers might not always use meters.

Cycle Rickshaws:

For short distances within the city, cycle rickshaws are another economical option. They are environmentally friendly and can navigate through narrow lanes and crowded streets.

Local Buses:

Amritsar has a network of local buses operated by the Punjab Roadways and PRTC (Punjab Road Transport Corporation). These buses cover various routes within the city and are a budget-friendly option for getting around.

Taxis:

Taxis are available for hire in Amritsar, and you can either book them through local taxi companies or find them at designated taxi stands. Taxis offer more comfort and privacy compared to auto rickshaws and cycle rickshaws but tend to be more expensive.

Walking:

Many of the attractions in Amritsar, such as the Golden Temple and Jallianwala Bagh, are located within walking distance of each other in the city center. Walking allows you to explore the streets at your own pace and take in the sights and sounds of Amritsar.
